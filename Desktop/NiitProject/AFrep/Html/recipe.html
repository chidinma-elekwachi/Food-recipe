Breakfast Options

Start your day with the flavors of Nigeria! Indulge in our classic Nigerian breakfast recipes that have been cherished for generations, bringing warmth and joy to families across the country.

Akara And Ogi

1. Soak peeled beans in water, then blend into a smooth batter.

2. Add chopped onions, peppers, and seasoning to the batter.

3. Heat oil in a frying pan and spoon the batter into the hot oil to make fritters.

4. Fry until golden brown and crispy, then drain on paper towels.

5. Serve the akara with a side of pap (ogi) for a delicious Nigerian breakfast.

Plantain and Egg

1. Peel ripe plantains and slice them diagonally into medium-sized pieces.

2. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the plantain slices until golden brown on both sides.

3. In another pan, scramble eggs with salt, pepper, and any desired seasonings.

4. Serve the fried plantains with the scrambled eggs for a delicious and satisfying breakfast.

Rice And Stew

1. Rinse rice thoroughly and cook until fluffy and tender.

2. In a separate pot, heat palm oil and sauté chopped onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers until fragrant.

3. Add tomato paste, crayfish, and stock cubes, then simmer until the stew thickens.

4. Serve the stew over the cooked rice for a classic Nigerian meal.

All Time Best Classic Nigerian Recipes

Take a trip down memory lane with our all-time favorite Nigerian dishes. These recipes have been enjoyed for generations, bringing joy to families across the country.

Jollofrice

1. Marinate thinly sliced beef or chicken in a mixture of ground peanuts, paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, ginger, and onion powder.

2. Skewer the marinated meat and grill over medium-high heat until cooked through, turning occasionally.

3. Serve hot with sliced onions, tomatoes, and spicy pepper sauce.

Small Chops

1. Prepare a variety of small bite-sized snacks such as puff puff, samosas, spring rolls, and chicken kebabs.

2. Serve with dipping sauces like pepper sauce, sweet chili sauce, or yogurt dip.

Fried Rice

1. Cook rice until slightly undercooked, then drain and set aside.

2. In a large skillet, sauté chopped onions, carrots, bell peppers, and peas in oil until tender.

3. Add cooked rice, soy sauce, and seasonings, then stir-fry until heated through.

SEASONAL RECIPES

Discover delicious recipes that match the current season. From comforting soups to refreshing salads, we've got something to suit the weather and your cravings.

Ofe Aku Soup

1. In a pot, combine chopped goat meat or beef with sliced cocoyam (taro) and enough water to cover.

2. Cook until the meat is tender and cocoyam is soft, then mash the cocoyam to thicken the soup.

3. Add palm oil, ground crayfish, ogiri (fermented locust beans), and bitter leaves.

4. Simmer for another 10-15 minutes, adjust seasoning if necessary, and serve hot with fufu or pounded yam.

Jollof Rice

1. Sauté chopped onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers in oil until softened.

2. Add tomato paste, curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, and stock cube, then cook until fragrant.

3. Stir in parboiled rice and enough water to cook, then simmer until rice is tender and flavorful.

Pepper Soup

1. In a pot, combine chopped goat meat or fish with water, chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and ground pepper soup spice.

2. Simmer over medium heat until the meat is tender and flavors are infused, about 30-45 minutes.

3. Serve hot with boiled yam, plantain, or rice.

FAV SNACKS

Treat yourself to a selection of mouthwatering Nigerian snacks that are perfect for any time of day. From crunchy chin chin to savory meat pies, these snacks are sure to hit the spot.

Pancake

1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

2. In another bowl, whisk together milk, eggs, and melted butter.

3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.

4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and pour batter onto the pan.

5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown.

6. Serve hot with maple syrup.

Buns

1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

2. Add melted butter and milk, then knead until a smooth dough forms.

3. Divide the dough into portions and shape into buns.

4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pot and fry the buns until golden brown.

5. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels.

6. Serve warm as a snack or with tea.

Chin Chin

1. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, and nutmeg.

2. Cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

3. Add milk and knead to form a stiff dough.

4. Roll out the dough and cut into small squares or desired shapes.

5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pot and fry the chin chin until golden brown.

DESSERTS

Stressed spelt backwards is DESSERTS!

Ice Cream

1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk, cream, sugar, and vanilla extract.

2. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to manufacturer's instructions.

3. Transfer the churned ice cream into a container and freeze until firm.

4. Serve scoops of ice cream in bowls or cones, optionally topped with sprinkles or chocolate sauce.

1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

2. Add melted butter and milk, then knead until a smooth dough forms.

3. Divide the dough into portions and shape into buns.

4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pot and fry the buns until golden brown.

5. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels.

6. Serve warm as a snack or with tea.

Groceries aka. Garri

1. In a bowl, mix garri (cassava flakes), groundnuts (peanuts), and sugar to taste.

2. Stir until well combined and serve as a crunchy and flavorful snack.

3. Enjoy with chilled water or your favorite beverage.
